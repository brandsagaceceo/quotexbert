## Kitchen Renovation Costs in Toronto (2026)

A kitchen renovation in Toronto in 2026 costs **$18,000 to $85,000+** depending on cabinet quality, countertop material, appliances, and whether you're reconfiguring the layout. The most common Toronto kitchen renovation budget is $30,000–$55,000 for a mid-range semi-custom renovation.

Here's why Toronto kitchen costs are high: skilled trades are expensive, Toronto labour rates lead Ontario, and a full kitchen renovation involves nearly every trade — plumbing, electrical, drywall, flooring, painting, and finish carpentry in addition to the cabinet installation.

## Toronto Kitchen Renovation Cost Tiers

### Tier 1: IKEA Kitchen Renovation — $18,000–$30,000

An IKEA-based kitchen renovation is the most affordable route to a fully updated kitchen in Toronto.

What's included at this price:
- IKEA PAX or SEKTION cabinets (factory-finished)
- Quartz or laminate countertops
- IKEA or mid-grade faucet and sink
- Basic tile backsplash
- Installation labour
- Permit costs

What it typically doesn't include:
- Appliances (add $3,000–$8,000 for a full appliance package)
- Structural changes or layout moves
- Custom hood fan or panel-ready appliance upgrades

IKEA cabinets have improved dramatically in quality. Paired with good countertops and hardware, an IKEA kitchen can look and function beautifully.

### Tier 2: Semi-Custom Kitchen Renovation — $32,000–$55,000

This is the most popular tier for Toronto homeowners doing a full kitchen renovation.

Semi-custom cabinets are made to order in a factory but with more size, finish, and configuration options than IKEA. Common brands in Toronto: Cabico, Nuform, Miralis, KitchenAid (Home Depot), and Thermoplastic.

### Tier 3: Full Custom Kitchen Renovation — $55,000–$120,000+

Full custom kitchens are built by local cabinet makers or specialty shops. Every dimension, finish, and detail is designed specifically for your space. Common in Toronto's higher-value neighbourhoods — Forest Hill, Rosedale, Leaside, Oakville, and Mississauga's premium areas.

At this level:
- Custom inset or overlay cabinetry (local craftsmen)
- Waterfall quartz or marble countertops ($150–$400 per linear ft)
- Under-cabinet lighting, smart lighting, hidden charging stations
- Professional-grade appliances (Wolf, Miele, Sub-Zero, Fisher & Paykel)
- Custom hood fan with professional ventilation
- Pot filler, wine fridge, drawer dishwasher options

## The Biggest Kitchen Renovation Cost Factors

### 1. Layout Changes

Moving the sink, dishwasher, or stove requires a plumber and electrician. **Moving a gas stove adds $1,500–$3,500**. Moving plumbing to create a kitchen island with a sink adds **$3,000–$6,000**.

### 2. Appliances

Appliances are separate from most kitchen renovation quotes. Budget separately:
- Basic package (LG, Whirlpool, Maytag): $3,500–$6,000
- Mid-range (Bosch, KitchenAid, Samsung): $6,000–$12,000
- Professional/luxury (Wolf, Miele, Sub-Zero): $18,000–$50,000+

### 3. Structural Work

Open-concept kitchen renovations in Toronto commonly require removing walls — some of which are load-bearing. **Removing a load-bearing wall costs $3,500–$12,000** including the structural engineer's report, beam, posts, and drywall repair.

### 4. Flooring

If you're replacing kitchen flooring as part of the renovation:
- LVP (luxury vinyl plank): $4–$9/sq ft installed
- Hardwood: $10–$18/sq ft installed
- Porcelain tile: $12–$24/sq ft installed

For a 200 sq ft kitchen: add $2,000–$5,500 for mid-range flooring.

## How to Get Accurate Kitchen Renovation Quotes

Kitchen renovation quotes in Toronto vary enormously — up to 50% between contractors. To protect yourself:

1. Get a minimum of 3 written, itemized quotes
2. Ensure all quotes cover the same scope (appliances, flooring, permits)
3. Ask each contractor for references and photos of similar kitchens
4. Verify WSIB clearance and liability insurance
