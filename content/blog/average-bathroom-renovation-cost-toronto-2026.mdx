## How Much Does a Bathroom Renovation Cost in Toronto?

In 2026, **bathroom renovations in Toronto cost an average of $18,000 to $32,000** for a standard full bathroom. But costs range from $7,000 for a simple powder room refresh to $65,000+ for a luxury master ensuite with high-end finishes.

Toronto has some of the highest renovation costs in Canada — skilled trades are in high demand, permit fees add up, and material costs have risen significantly since 2022. This guide breaks down every cost component so you can budget accurately.

## What Drives Bathroom Renovation Costs in Toronto?

### 1. Plumbing Work

Plumbing is often the biggest variable in bathroom renovation cost. **Moving plumbing fixtures adds $2,500–$8,000** to a renovation. If you can keep the toilet, sink, and shower/tub in the same position, you'll save significantly versus reconfiguring the entire layout.

Expect to pay $800–$2,000 just for the licensed plumber's rough-in and hook-up work even if you keep everything in place.

### 2. Tile Work

Tile selection and installation is often the most visible (and most varied) cost category in a bathroom renovation.

- Ceramic tile installation: $8–$15/sq ft installed
- Porcelain tile installation: $12–$22/sq ft installed  
- Natural stone (marble, travertine): $25–$50/sq ft installed
- Large format tile (24x24 or larger): Add 15–25% labour premium

A standard 50 sq ft bathroom floor with mid-range porcelain tile runs **$1,500–$3,000 installed** in Toronto.

### 4. Labour Costs in Toronto

Toronto labour rates for bathroom renovations are among the highest in Ontario:

- General contractor markup: 20–30%
- Tile setter: $75–$120/hr
- Plumber: $130–$175/hr
- Electrician: $110–$160/hr
- Drywall/painting: $55–$80/hr

On a $20,000 bathroom renovation, expect roughly $8,000–$10,000 to be labour.

### 5. Permits

City of Toronto building permits for bathroom renovations range from **$300–$800** depending on scope. If you're adding a new bathroom where there wasn't one before, permits may run higher.

Always pull permits — unpermitted bathroom work can cause serious complications when you sell or refinance.

## How to Save on a Toronto Bathroom Renovation

1. **Keep the layout** — don't move plumbing. This is the biggest savings opportunity.
2. **Mid-range tile** — large format porcelain at $3–$6/tile looks premium without the marble price tag.
3. **Keep the tub surround as tile** — full walk-in rainfall showers are beautiful but add $8,000–$15,000.
4. **Buy your own fixtures** — contractor-supplied fixtures carry a 20–35% markup. Shop at Costco, IKEA, or specialty importers.
5. **Get 3 quotes** — Toronto bathroom renovation prices vary by up to 40% between contractors. Always compare 3 detailed written quotes.
