## Oshawa vs Toronto: Bathroom Renovation Cost Comparison

Thinking about renovating your bathroom? If you live in Durham Region, you might save **15-25% compared to downtown Toronto** by hiring local Oshawa contractors. Here's the complete cost breakdown.

## Average Full Bathroom Renovation

### Oshawa/Durham Region
- **Small (5x8)**: $8,000 - $12,000
- **Medium (8x10)**: $12,000 - $18,000
- **Large (10x12)**: $18,000 - $28,000

### Downtown Toronto
- **Small (5x8)**: $10,000 - $15,000
- **Medium (8x10)**: $15,000 - $22,000
- **Large (10x12)**: $22,000 - $35,000

**Savings in Oshawa**: $2,000 - $7,000 depending on project size

## Why Oshawa Costs Less

### 1. Lower Labor Rates
- **Oshawa plumbers**: $85-110/hour
- **Toronto plumbers**: $110-150/hour

- **Oshawa tilers**: $45-65/sq ft
- **Toronto tilers**: $55-80/sq ft

- **Oshawa electricians**: $80-100/hour
- **Toronto electricians**: $100-140/hour

### 2. Material Availability
Durham Region has excellent big-box store coverage (Home Depot, Lowe's, Kent) without Toronto's delivery surcharges.

### 3. Permit Costs
- **Oshawa Building Department**: $200-400
- **Toronto Building**: $300-600

### 4. Parking & Access
No downtown parking nightmares = Lower contractor overhead = Lower prices for you

Despite higher costs, Toronto offers:

For luxury renovations ($40K+), Toronto specialists may be worthwhile.

## Hidden Costs to Watch

**Oshawa-Specific:**
- Older homes (pre-1970) often need new plumbing stacks
- Asbestos testing/removal (common in older Oshawa homes)
- Ventilation upgrades (Ontario code changes)

**Toronto-Specific:**
- Condo board approvals (adds 2-4 weeks)
- Elevator booking fees ($200-500)
- Street parking permits for contractors
