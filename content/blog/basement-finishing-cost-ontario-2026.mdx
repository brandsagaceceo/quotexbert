## Basement Finishing Costs in Ontario (2026)

Finishing a basement in Ontario in 2026 costs **$35,000 to $100,000+** depending on scope, location, and whether you're creating a legal secondary suite. The province-wide average for a standard 800–1,000 sq ft basement finishing project sits around **$55,000–$75,000** in the GTA, and $40,000–$60,000 in other Ontario cities.

## What Affects Basement Finishing Costs in Ontario?

The wide price range comes down to five key variables:

### 1. Location within Ontario

Labour is the biggest driver of basement cost variation across Ontario:

| Location | Basic Finishing (800 sq ft) | Mid-Range | Full Legal Suite |
|----------|---------------------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Toronto / Inner GTA | $45,000–$65,000 | $65,000–$90,000 | $80,000–$130,000 |
| Outer GTA (Brampton, Mississauga, Oshawa) | $38,000–$55,000 | $55,000–$75,000 | $70,000–$110,000 |
| Hamilton, Kitchener-Waterloo | $32,000–$48,000 | $48,000–$68,000 | $60,000–$95,000 |
| London, Windsor | $28,000–$42,000 | $42,000–$60,000 | $55,000–$85,000 |
| Ottawa | $35,000–$52,000 | $52,000–$72,000 | $65,000–$100,000 |

### 2. Existing Basement Condition

The condition of your unfinished basement dramatically affects cost:

- **Dry basement, already framed**: Save $5,000–$15,000
- **Underpinning required** (ceiling too low): Add $30,000–$80,000
- **Waterproofing needed**: Add $8,000–$25,000
- **Mold remediation**: Add $2,500–$15,000
- **Asbestos removal** (pre-1990 homes): Add $3,000–$12,000

Before budgeting your project, have a contractor inspect for these issues.

### 3. Ceiling Height

Ontario Building Code requires a minimum ceiling height of **1.95 metres (6'5")** for habitable space (mandatory for a legal basement apartment).

If your current basement ceiling is below 6'5", you have two options:
- **Underpinning**: Excavating and deepening the foundation. Costs $30,000–$80,000 but adds long-term value.
- **Bench footings**: Less invasive, reduces usable floor area. Costs $15,000–$35,000.

### 4. Legal Suite vs. Recreational Space

Creating a **legal secondary suite** (with kitchen, full bathroom, separate entrance, egress windows) costs $25,000–$45,000 more than basic recreation space — but generates $1,600–$2,800/month in rental income (GTA average).

Legal suite additions vs. basic space:
- Full bathroom: +$14,000–$22,000
- Kitchen: +$12,000–$22,000
- Egress windows (2–3 required): +$3,000–$7,000
- Separate entrance modification: +$5,000–$12,000
- Additional permits and inspections: +$2,000–$5,000

### 5. Finishes and Materials

Material choices drive the widest range in costs:

| Item | Budget | Mid-Range | Premium |
|------|--------|-----------|---------|
| Flooring (800 sq ft) | $3,500 (LVP) | $6,000 (Eng. Hardwood) | $9,500 (Tile) |
| Bathroom (3-pc) | $8,000 | $14,000 | $22,000 |
| Wet Bar | $3,000 | $7,000 | $15,000 |
| Fireplace (electric) | $1,200 | $2,800 | $5,500 |

## Ontario Basement Finishing Cost Breakdown (Per Component)

### Framing and Insulation
- Framing (exterior walls, partition walls): $3,000–$7,500
- Spray foam insulation (exterior walls): $2,500–$5,000
- Batt insulation alternative: $1,500–$3,000
- Vapour barrier: $800–$1,500

### Electrical
- Rough-in and panel upgrade (if needed): $3,500–$7,000
- Pot lights, outlets, switches: $2,000–$4,500
- Dedicated circuits (home theatre, gym): +$500–$1,500 each

### Plumbing
- Bathroom rough-in (3-piece): $3,500–$6,000
- Bathroom fixtures and installation: $4,500–$10,000
- Kitchenette or wet bar plumbing: $2,000–$5,000

### Drywall and Paint
- Drywall, tape, mud, prime: $4,000–$8,000
- Paint (2 coats, full basement): $1,500–$3,000

### Flooring
- LVP (most popular for basements): $4–$8/sq ft installed
- Engineered hardwood: $8–$14/sq ft installed
- Carpet: $4–$7/sq ft installed

## Ontario Basement ROI: Is It Worth It?

### Return on Investment (Toronto and GTA):
- **Legal basement suite**: ROI of 70–100%+ over 5 years when rented
- **Recreational space (family room, home office)**: 60–80% cost recovered at resale
- **Home gym**: 40–60% cost recovered

In Toronto's real estate market, a **professionally finished basement adds $80,000–$150,000 to home value** at resale — often exceeding the renovation cost in higher-demand areas.
