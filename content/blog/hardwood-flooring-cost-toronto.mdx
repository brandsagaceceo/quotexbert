## Hardwood Flooring Costs in Toronto & GTA (2026)

Hardwood flooring remains the gold standard for Toronto homes, adding beauty and value. Here's what you'll pay in 2026.

## Average Costs (Installed)

**Solid Hardwood:**
- Red Oak 3.25": $10-14/sq ft
- White Oak 5": $13-18/sq ft
- Maple: $11-15/sq ft
- Brazilian Cherry: $15-22/sq ft

**Engineered Hardwood:**
- Basic (3-ply): $8-11/sq ft
- Premium (7-ply): $11-15/sq ft
- Wide plank: $13-18/sq ft

**For 500 sq ft (avg bedroom + hallway):**
- **Solid oak**: $5,000 - $7,000
- **Engineered**: $4,000 - $6,500

## Cost Breakdown

### Materials Only
- **Solid hardwood**: $5-10/sq ft
- **Engineered**: $4-8/sq ft
- **Underlayment**: $0.50-1/sq ft
- **Finishing**: $2-4/sq ft (if unfinished)

### Labor (Toronto/GTA)
- **Installation**: $3-6/sq ft
- **Floor prep**: $1-2/sq ft
- **Removal of old flooring**: $1.50-3/sq ft
- **Stairs**: $75-150 per step

### Where to Buy (GTA)
- Lumber Liquidators (Mississauga, Markham)
- Alexanian Carpet & Flooring (Multiple locations)
- Home Depot/Lowe's (Budget options)
- Local hardwood dealers (Best selection)

## Solid vs Engineered Hardwood

### Solid Hardwood
**Pros:**
- Can be refinished 3-5 times (lasts 50+ years)
- Authentic feel
- Increases home value most

**Cons:**
- Not for basements (moisture sensitive)
- More expensive
- Takes 3-5 days to install + finish

**Best for:** Main floors, bedrooms, Toronto heritage homes

### Engineered Hardwood
**Pros:**
- Works in basements
- Faster installation (1-2 days)
- Pre-finished options
- More stable in humidity changes

**Cons:**
- Can only refinish 1-2 times
- Thinner wear layer
- Lower resale impact

**Best for:** Basements, condos, over radiant heat

## Red Oak vs White Oak

### Red Oak (Most Popular in Toronto)
- **Cost**: $10-14/sq ft installed
- **Appearance**: Pink/red tones, prominent grain
- **Hardness**: 1290 Janka (good durability)
- **Staining**: Takes stain very well

### White Oak (Trending)
- **Cost**: $13-18/sq ft installed
- **Appearance**: Gray/brown tones, tighter grain
- **Hardness**: 1360 Janka (harder than red oak)
- **Moisture**: Better for basements (denser)
- **Style**: Modern, Scandinavian looks

**Toronto trend (2026)**: White oak in matte/natural finish gaining popularity

## Installation Methods

### Nail-Down (Most Common)
- **Cost**: $3-4/sq ft labor
- **Time**: 2-3 days (500 sq ft)
- **Best for**: Solid hardwood over plywood subfloor
- **Noise**: Can be loud during install

### Glue-Down
- **Cost**: $4-5/sq ft labor
- **Time**: 2-3 days
- **Best for**: Engineered over concrete (basements)
- **Pros**: Very stable, quiet install

### Floating
- **Cost**: $2-3/sq ft labor
- **Time**: 1-2 days
- **Best for**: DIY, rentals, temporary
- **Cons**: Less stable, can shift

## Pre-Finished vs Site-Finished

### Pre-Finished Hardwood
- **Cost**: +$1-2/sq ft premium
- **Time**: 1-2 days install, walk on immediately
- **Finish**: Factory UV-cured (very durable)
- **Cons**: Slight bevels between boards

### Site-Finished (Unfinished)
- **Cost**: Cheaper materials, but +$2-4/sq ft finishing
- **Time**: 3-5 days (install + 3 coats + dry time)
- **Finish**: Seamless, custom color
- **Smell**: Strong fumes during finishing (leave house 2-3 days)

**Toronto recommendation**: Pre-finished for convenience, site-finished for custom color matching

## Subfloor Preparation

**Common issues in Toronto homes (pre-1980):**
- Uneven concrete (basements)
- Squeaky subfloors
- Old adhesive removal
- Asbestos tiles underneath

**Prep costs:**
- **Leveling**: $2-5/sq ft
- **Plywood overlay**: $3-6/sq ft
- **Squeak repair**: $200-500
- **Asbestos removal**: $4-8/sq ft (licensed required)

> **Tip**: Get subfloor inspected BEFORE buying flooring. Bad subfloors can add $2,000-5,000 to your project.

## Timeline

**Pre-finished solid hardwood (500 sq ft):**
- Day 1: Remove old flooring, prep subfloor
- Day 2-3: Install hardwood
- Day 4: Install baseboards, clean
- **Total**: 4-5 days

**Site-finished:**
- Add 3-4 days for sanding, staining, 3 coats polyurethane

## Cost-Saving Tips

1. **Buy during sales**: March/September typically 15-20% off
2. **Pre-finished**: Saves $1,000-2,000 in finishing costs
3. **Red oak over white oak**: Save $3-4/sq ft
4. **Keep old baseboards**: Saves $2-3/sq ft
5. **DIY demolition**: Save $750-1,500 (500 sq ft)
6. **Engineered in basement**: Save $2-4/sq ft vs solid

## Finding Installers (Toronto/GTA)

**Verify:**
- ✅ WSIB coverage
- ✅ Insurance ($2M)
- ✅ Portfolio of Toronto homes (similar to yours)
- ✅ References (check Google reviews)
- ✅ Warranty (1 year minimum on install)

**Red flags:**
- ❌ Cash only
- ❌ Asks for >50% deposit
- ❌ Won't inspect subfloor first
- ❌ Quotes way below market (30%+ lower)

**Get 3-4 quotes**. Reasonable range: Within 20% of each other

## Permits

**No permit required** for flooring replacement in Toronto/GTA.

**Exception**: If you're altering floor joists or structural elements during prep, permit needed.

## ROI & Resale Value

**Toronto/GTA market:**
- Hardwood floors expected in homes $750K+
- Adds $5,000-12,000 to home value (depending on quality)
- ROI: 70-90% of cost
- Homes with hardwood sell 12% faster

**Best ROI**: Red oak on main floor of detached home in Toronto/Durham

## Maintenance Costs

- **Annual cleaning**: $200-400 (professional)
- **Refinishing**: $3-5/sq ft every 10-15 years
- **Minor repairs**: $150-400

**Total 20-year cost**: Initial install + $2,000-4,000 maintenance

**Still cheaper than**: Replacing carpet 3 times ($12,000-18,000)

## Conclusion

Hardwood flooring in Toronto costs **$8-18/sq ft installed** depending on species and finish. For a typical 500 sq ft project, budget $4,000-9,000 including installation.
